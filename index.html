Milk.





Milk's health benefits are many and the ways that milk is distributed help to give people all over oportunities to drink it. 226 billion pounds of milk are made each year by milk farmers in the USA alone. Each cow in the USA milk farming industry makes around 24,000 pounds of milk per year. Each year 15,000 tractor trailers bring milk around the USA. Each of these tractor trailers has a maximum fuel capacity of 300 gallons in their fuel tanks. Meaning, at minimum, they would burn 4.5 million gallons of gas per year if each tractor trailer were to only run on a single tank of gas. In reality the true number is probably much higher- maybe in the 10s or hundreads of millions. You can do your part in helping with carbon emissions by buying local produce and meats. By doing this you reduce the amount of area a truck has to travel to deliver its packages.

Scientists have found evidence of the use of animal milk to feed infants as far back as 8000 years ago. Today, milk is a very important part of school lunches. Milk is required to only be served in low fat form in schools because of Michelle Obama's "Healthy Hunger Free Kids" act of 2010. Full fat milk has a higher cholesterol level than low fat milk, making low fat milk more desirable for school lunches. Even with this fact, milk farmers still advocate for the re-entry of full fat milk to school lunches. They say that low fat milk tastes worse than full fat milk. They claim it is hurting the consumer market to serve kids less tasty milk, because future generations will not be as incline to buy as much milk as they would if they grew up with good tasting milk. Low fat milks have a higher amount of vitamins than full fat milks because of fortification. Fortification is when the milk producer adds extra vitamins and minerals to the milk they are making. This is not mandated by law but it is an extra selling point so it's common to see more vitamins in milk that would not normally be there.

In addition to its health benefits, milk is also commonly used in baking. When you add milk to dough and heat the dough it triggers the maillard reaction. This can be achived with water but at a lesser degree. Using milk in the maillard reaction helps with cooling the bread faster, making the bread taste better and smell better. It also helps with the browning of the bread crust. This happens because of the amino acids in the food and the reducing of sugars. You can trigger the maillard reaction by heating your dough to 284 degrees and 330 degrees. If you go over 330 degrees the bread starts to burn and char over. So, make sure to keep the heat within the sweet spot.
